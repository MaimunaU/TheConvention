Food Booths

Tteokbokki and Eomuk

These are a popular Korean street food pair. Tteokbokki is spicy rice cakes and eomuk is fish cakes. You can eat them separately or together, but they are a must have snack.

Kimbap

Kimbap is Korean seaweed rice rolls. Kimbap can have various fillings, but are typically filled with carrots, radish, and other crunchy vegetables. Aside from these vegatables, other ingridents like, meat, fish, cheese, and kimchi are also added in. You can customize your own gimbap or order the versions on the menu.

Soondae

Soondae are blood sausages. They are made with various meats but typically have a base of pork blood, cellophane noodles, and glutinous rice. It has a strong taste of blood, so it's okay if it isn't your thing.

Bungeoppang

Bungeoppang is fish-shaped bread with a sweet, creamy filling. It has a crispy waffle outside and is traditionaly filled with a sweet red-bean paste. However, there are other fillings like, custard cream or nutella. This is best when eaten hot.

Hotteok

Hotteok are sweet pancakes with a sweet filling. The filling is made of brown sugar, honey, peanuts, and cinnamon. Just like bungeoppang, hotteok is best when hot.
